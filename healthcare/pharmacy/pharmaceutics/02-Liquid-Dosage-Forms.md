## 1 Introduction

- [ ] Liquid dispersion system (**API** [Active Pharmaceutic Ingredient] dispersed
      in liquid medium).
- [ ] Classification

  - Administration routes:

    => Oral use \
    => External use like skin, eye, nose, ear, mouth, rectum, vagina \
    => All needs **sterile** preparations

  - Dosage system:

    => Homogeneous phase, like low molecular solutions and macromolecular solutions \
    => Heterogeneous phase, sols, emulsions, suspensions

- [ ] Characteristics

  - Advantages:

    => Highly dispersed, rapid absorption & effect: **solutions > colloids >
    emulsions > suspensions** \
    => Broad administration routes \
    => Easily divided into small dosage for children and elders \
    => Decrease irritation \
    => Improve bio-availability

  - Disadvantages:

    => Chemical stability \
    => Physical stability \
    => Volume \
    => Mildew and rancidity

## 2 Surfactants (Surface-active agents)

- [ ] **Surface tension**: attractive forces that at the surface and in the
      bulk of a material.
- [ ] **Surfactants**:

  - High surface activity, significantly decrease the surface tension of liquid
  - Structure properties: **hydrophilic groups** as heads and **hydrophobic groups**
    as tails.

    ```smiles
    CCCCCCCCC=CCCCCCCCC(=O)OCCOCC(C1C(C(CO1)OCCO)OCCO)OCCO
    ```

    => Polysorbate-80 has hydrophilic groups `OCCO` (**polyoxyethylene**) and
    it also has hydrophobic groups `COC(=O)CCCCCCCC=CCCCCCCCC` (**monooleate**)

  - Classification

    => **Anionic** (阴离子型), $\color{yellow}{\text{CH}_3(\text{CH}_2)_{10}}$-
    $\color{lime}{\text{COO}^-\text{Na}^+}$ \
    => **Cationic** (阳离子型), $\color{yellow}{\text{CH}_3}(\text{CH}_2)_{15}$-
    $\color{lime}{\text{N}^+(\text{CH}_3)_2\text{CH}_3\text{Br}^-}$ \
    => **Non-ionic** (非离子型) \
    => **Ampholytic** (两性型)

- [ ] Surface activity: **Decrease surface tension**
- [ ] **Micelles** (胶束)

  <div style="display: flex; justify-content: center;">
    <img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/44/CMC.pdf/page1-337px-CMC.pdf.jpg" style="width: 30%">
  </div>

  - At low concentration (< CMC): surfactant molecules acts alone, and they
    mainly stay at the surface of water (hydrophobic tails pointing to sky).

    | properties                                       |   growth magnitude   |
    | :----------------------------------------------- | :------------------: |
    | **osmotic pressure**                             |  $\uparrow$ rapidly  |
    | **surface tension**                              | $\downarrow$ rapidly |
    | **molar conductivity**                           | $\downarrow$ slowly  |
    | **solubility of a water-insoluble solubilizate** |   almost no change   |
    | **intensity of light scattered by the solution** |   almost no change   |

  - At high concentration (> CMC): when CMC is reached, the surface is already
    filled with molecules, and if the concentration is increased, the molecules
    cannot stay on the surface. To avoid water molecules, their hydrophobic tails
    spontaneously **clump** together to form spherical

    | properties                                       |   growth magnitude   |
    | :----------------------------------------------- | :------------------: |
    | **osmotic pressure**                             |  $\uparrow$ slowly   |
    | **surface tension**                              | $\downarrow$ slowly  |
    | **molar conductivity**                           | $\downarrow$ rapidly |
    | **solubility of a water-insoluble solubilizate** |  $\uparrow$ rapidly  |

    => Osmotic pressure depends on the **number of free particles in the solution**.
    After the micelle is formed, hundreds of thousands of molecules polymerize
    into a micelle, and the number of particles increases slowly.

    => The arrangement of surfactant molecules on the surface of the water reduces
    surface tension. When the surface is full, **excess molecules enter the inside
    of the solution to form micelles**, so the surface tension almost no longer
    drops.

    => After the formation of micelles of ionic surfactants, the charge density
    changes, and some **counterions are absorbed on the surface of the micelles**,
    resulting in a decrease in the effective charge and a decrease in mobility.

    => When c > CMC, micelles begin to form in the system. Poorly soluble
    substances (usually hydrophobic) enter the hydrophobic core of the micelle
    or are distributed in the fence layer of the micelle. This phenomenon is
    known as **micellar solubilization**

    => The size of micelles belongs to the **colloidal category** and produces
    a pronounced **Tyndall effect** (light scattering), while monomer molecules
    are too small and the scattering is weak.

- [ ] Structures of micelles

  - **Double electric layer structure** (双层电荷结构): ionic surfactants
  - **Palisade structure** (栅栏结构): non-ionic surfactants

  | shape                      | formation                                                                                          |
  | :------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
  | **sphere**                 | The most basic form                                                                                |
  | **ellipsoid**/**cylinder** | As the concentration increases further, the spherical micelles will fuse and elongate              |
  | **planar structure**:      | A layered arrangement of sheets formed at large concentrations, similar to a palisade structure    |
  | **unilamellar vesicle**    | A closed sphere made of a double layer of surfactant with a water nucleus wrapped around the water |

  - **Reversed Micelle** (反胶束): Present in organic solvents. The **hydrophilic
    groups** head base points to the inside to wrap a very small amount of water,
    and the **hydrophobic groups** tail points outward to the organic solvent.

- [ ] There is actually natural surfactant: **bile salts** (胆汁盐)

  - It is similar to artificial surfactants, bile salt molecules have a hydrophobic
    side (convex side) and a hydrophilic side (concave side).
  - They are secreted by the **liver** and stored in the gallbaldder, responsible
    for emulsifying large oil droplets in food into tiny droplets.

  1. **Formation of mixed micelles**: bile salt + fatty acids and **glycerol**
    (甘油一酯) -> small mixed micelles;
  2. **Overcoming the water barrier**: There is an unstirred water layer on the
    surface of **intestinal epithelial cells** (肠道上皮细胞), through which pure
    oil cannot pass through. Micelles use their outer hydrophilic groups to
    encapsulate the hydrophobic fat components inside, passing through the water
    layer like a **boat**;
  3. **Simple diffusion**: When micelles come into contact with the plasma
    membrane, the fatty acids and monoglycerides inside enter the cell through
    simple diffusion.

    -> **Repackaging**: Inside the cell, these ingredients are resynthesized
    into **triglyceride**.
  
  4. **Chylomicrons and lymphatic transport**: Cells put a protein coat on
    triglycerides, forming larger particles called **chylomicron** (乳糜微粒).

    -> Chylomicrons leave the cell by **exocytosis** (胞吐作用). Because they
    are too large to enter the **capillary** (毛细血管), they enter the larger
    diameter of the **lacteal** (乳糜管).
